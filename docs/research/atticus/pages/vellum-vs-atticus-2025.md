# Vellum vs Atticus: Which Book Formatting Software Is Worth Your Money in 2025?

Written by [brighterapublishing.com](https://brighterapublishing.com/author/fivemilestonesllcgmail-com/)

October 12, 2025

Estimated reading time: 16 minutes

You have finished writing your book. The manuscript is edited, the cover is designed, and you are ready to format it for publication. Then you face the formatting decision that divides the self publishing community into passionate camps: Vellum or Atticus? Both promise professional formatting without requiring design expertise. Both claim to save hours compared to manual formatting. Both cost real money in a world where free alternatives exist.

The choice matters because formatting determines how readers experience your book. Poor formatting distracts from your story, generates negative reviews, and makes your work look amateurish regardless of the writing quality. Professional formatting disappears, allowing your words to shine without technical issues interrupting the reading experience. Getting this right from the start saves the headache of reformatting later and republishing across multiple platforms.

Here is what makes this decision particularly challenging: both Vellum and Atticus are genuinely excellent tools that produce professional results. Neither is a bad choice. The question is not which one works but which one works better for your specific needs, budget, workflow, and operating system. Let’s break down exactly what each offers so you can invest your money wisely.

## The Quick Answer for Authors in a Hurry

If you are a Mac user publishing fiction with straightforward formatting needs and want the easiest, most polished experience available, Vellum remains the gold standard. If you use Windows, publish nonfiction requiring complex layouts, want writing and editing features alongside formatting, or prefer a one time purchase over separate ebook and print packages, Atticus deserves serious consideration. Both will make your book look professional.

## Understanding Vellum: The Established Favorite

Vellum launched in 2015 and quickly became the formatting tool serious indie authors recommended without hesitation. The software does one thing exceptionally well: it takes your manuscript and transforms it into beautiful ebook and print files with minimal effort and maximum polish. The interface is intuitive, the output is consistently professional, and the learning curve is remarkably gentle.

**The Mac Only Limitation**

Before discussing anything else, address the elephant in the room: Vellum only works on Mac computers. If you use Windows or Linux, Vellum is not an option unless you are willing to rent cloud access to a Mac through services like MacInCloud, which adds ongoing costs and complexity. This single limitation eliminates Vellum for a significant portion of self published authors.

For Mac users, this exclusivity actually provides benefits. The software takes full advantage of Mac’s interface conventions, integrations, and design philosophy. It feels native rather than like a cross platform compromise. Everything works exactly how Mac users expect, creating a seamless experience.

**Pricing Structure: Pay for What You Need**

Vellum uses a tiered pricing model based on which formats you need. The Ebook Only version costs $249.99, creating ebook files for Kindle, Apple Books, Kobo, and other retailers. The Print Only version costs the same $249.99, generating print ready PDFs for paperbacks. The Everything Bundle combining both costs $499.99, saving you money if you need both formats.

This pricing structure lets authors starting with ebooks only invest $250 rather than $500, knowing they can upgrade later if they add print editions. However, it also means many authors eventually pay the full $500 to access both features. There are no subscription fees or ongoing costs. Buy once, use forever, including all future updates.

Updates have been substantial and free since launch. Vellum regularly adds new style options, improves features, and refines the interface without charging upgrade fees. Authors who purchased Vellum in 2015 still access the latest 2025 features at no additional cost. This makes the upfront investment more palatable when you consider years of use without recurring charges.

**The Formatting Experience: Simplicity Meets Beauty**

Vellum’s interface is divided into three main sections: the manuscript editor, the style customizer, and the preview pane. Import your Word document or paste your text directly. Choose from dozens of professionally designed style templates covering every major genre. Customize fonts, chapter headings, ornamental breaks, and other design elements with simple dropdown menus and sliders. Preview your book exactly as readers will see it across different devices.

The beauty is how much Vellum handles automatically. Drop in chapter headings using simple formatting in your manuscript and Vellum recognizes them, applies consistent styling, and builds your table of contents automatically. Add front matter like dedication and acknowledgments with a few clicks. Create back matter with author bio and links to your other books. Everything formats consistently without requiring design knowledge.

Style templates are thoughtfully designed and genre appropriate. Romance novels get elegant, flowing designs. Thrillers look crisp and urgent. Literary fiction feels refined and understated. Nonfiction appears clean and readable. Each template is fully customizable, but the defaults are so polished that many authors use them with minimal tweaking.

**What Vellum Does Brilliantly**

Speed is Vellum’s superpower. Authors who previously spent hours or days manually formatting in Word or fighting with other tools can format a full novel in under an hour once they learn the workflow. Many authors report formatting books in 30 minutes after becoming comfortable with the software. This time savings alone justifies the cost for authors publishing multiple books annually.

The output quality is consistently excellent. Files work flawlessly across Kindle, Apple Books, Kobo, and other platforms without the technical issues that plague manually formatted books. Print PDFs meet exact specifications for KDP, IngramSpark, and other print on demand services. You generate files, upload, and they work without corrections or rejections.

Ebook formatting includes intelligent features like automatically linking footnotes, creating clickable table of contents, handling images properly, and ensuring consistent spacing throughout. These details separate amateur ebooks from professional ones. Vellum handles them all automatically.

Print formatting is equally impressive. Vellum manages page breaks, running headers, page numbers, and all the technical requirements that make print formatting intimidating. Choose your trim size, set your margins, select whether to include running headers, and Vellum generates print ready PDFs that meet professional standards.

Updates and improvements continue regularly. Recent additions include expanded style customization, better nonfiction support, enhanced image handling, and improved subtitle formatting. The development team actively listens to author feedback and implements requested features.

**Where Vellum Falls Short**

Nonfiction formatting with complex requirements pushes Vellum’s limits. Authors publishing books with extensive footnotes, sidebars, tables, charts, or complex multi column layouts find Vellum less capable than dedicated page layout software like Adobe InDesign. The software handles straightforward nonfiction well but struggles with highly designed instructional books, textbooks, or coffee table books.

Writing and editing happen elsewhere. Vellum is exclusively a formatting tool. You cannot write your manuscript in Vellum. The built in editor handles basic edits during formatting, but you will do your actual writing in Scrivener, Word, Google Docs, or another writing app. For authors who prefer all in one solutions, this separation creates extra steps.

The Mac requirement remains the biggest limitation. Roughly 80% of computer users run Windows. Unless they own a Mac, borrow one, or rent cloud access, Vellum is simply unavailable. This excludes the majority of potential users regardless of how good the software is.

Collaboration features are minimal. Vellum works as a single user application on one computer. Sharing projects with editors, formatters, or co authors requires manually sharing files. Teams working together find this workflow cumbersome compared to cloud based alternatives.

## Understanding Atticus: The Rising Challenger

Atticus launched in 2021 as the first serious competitor offering Vellum like ease with cross platform availability and additional features. The software combines writing, editing, formatting, and publishing tools in one application that works on both Mac and Windows. For authors who resented Vellum’s Mac exclusivity, Atticus answered prayers.

**Cross Platform Freedom**

Atticus’s biggest selling point is simple: it works on Windows and Mac equally well. Authors using either operating system access identical features and interfaces. This levels the playing field and expands options for the majority of authors using Windows who previously had no equivalent to Vellum.

The software is entirely cloud based. Log in from any computer with internet access and your projects sync automatically. Start formatting on your desktop, make edits on your laptop, check progress from a borrowed computer. This flexibility appeals to authors who work across multiple devices or travel frequently.

**Pricing: One Price for Everything**

Atticus uses straightforward pricing: $147 one time for lifetime access to everything. This includes ebook formatting, print formatting, writing tools, and all future updates. No separate packages. No choosing between ebook and print. Pay once, access all features forever.

The price advantage is significant. Atticus costs $147 compared to Vellum’s $499.99 for equivalent ebook and print capabilities. For budget conscious authors or those publishing their first book, this $352 difference is substantial. The savings could cover editing, cover design, or marketing expenses instead.

Like Vellum, Atticus has no subscription fees. Your one time purchase includes unlimited books, unlimited formats, and all future feature additions. The development team ships regular updates adding functionality without charging existing users.

**The Formatting Experience: Familiar Yet Different**

Atticus’s interface intentionally echoes Vellum’s successful design while adding unique elements. You see your manuscript on the left, formatting controls in the middle, and preview on the right. The layout feels immediately familiar to anyone who has used Vellum.

Import manuscripts from Word, Google Docs, Scrivener, or write directly in Atticus. Choose from style templates covering major genres. Customize fonts, spacing, chapter styling, and decorative elements. Generate ebook and print files with clicks. The fundamental workflow mirrors Vellum while adding features Vellum lacks.

Style templates in Atticus are well designed and cover fiction and nonfiction comprehensively. They are not quite as refined or numerous as Vellum’s templates, but they are completely serviceable and produce professional results. Most authors will find templates suitable for their genres.

**Beyond Formatting: The Writing Environment**

Atticus distinguishes itself by including a full featured writing environment. You can write your entire manuscript in Atticus, eliminating the need for separate writing software. The writing interface includes distraction free modes, word count tracking, chapter organization, and note keeping.

For authors who prefer streamlined workflows, writing and formatting in one application saves time and context switching. Draft your novel in Atticus, make edits as you go, then click over to formatting mode to prepare publication files. Everything lives in one place.

The editor includes basic manuscript editing tools like find and replace, spell check, and formatting cleanup. These are not as robust as dedicated editing software like ProWritingAid or Grammarly, but they handle everyday editing tasks adequately.

**What Atticus Does Brilliantly**

Value is Atticus’s strongest selling point. For $147 you get ebook formatting, print formatting, and writing tools that would cost $500+ separately through Vellum and writing software. Authors on tight budgets or publishing their first books find this pricing structure far more accessible.

Windows compatibility opens professional formatting to the majority of authors who were previously relegated to manual formatting, expensive professionals, or clunky free tools. This alone makes Atticus significant for democratizing professional book production.

Nonfiction support is stronger in Atticus than Vellum. The software handles footnotes, endnotes, sidebars, and more complex layouts better. Authors publishing business books, memoirs with extensive notes, or instructional content find Atticus more capable for their formatting needs.

The all in one approach appeals to authors who prefer fewer applications. Write, edit, format, and generate publication files without switching between Scrivener, Word, Vellum, and other tools. Everything happens in one interface.

Updates have been impressive. Since launching in 2021, Atticus has added features rapidly based on user feedback. The development team is responsive, communicative, and clearly committed to improving the software continuously.

**Where Atticus Falls Short**

Template polish does not quite match Vellum’s refinement. Atticus templates are good, but Vellum’s are exceptional. Authors with keen eyes for design notice the difference. The gap is narrowing with each Atticus update, but Vellum still edges ahead in pure aesthetic excellence.

The interface, while functional, feels slightly less intuitive than Vellum’s ultra polished experience. Atticus users adapt quickly, but the learning curve is marginally steeper. Small interface quirks that Mac users notice occasionally interrupt workflow in ways Vellum’s native Mac design avoids.

Output quality is excellent but occasionally requires troubleshooting that Vellum files rarely need. Most Atticus generated files upload perfectly, but some users report needing to adjust settings or regenerate files to resolve platform specific issues. These are exceptions rather than rules, but they occur more frequently than with Vellum.

Being newer means Atticus has less track record. Vellum has nearly a decade of proven reliability and continuous development. Atticus is only a few years old. While the development team seems committed long term, newer software always carries slightly more uncertainty about future support.

## Head to Head: The Details That Matter

**Ease of Use Winner: Vellum**

Both applications are remarkably user friendly, but Vellum edges ahead in pure intuitiveness. The interface is cleaner, the workflow is more obvious, and the design polish makes every interaction feel effortless. Atticus is genuinely easy to learn, but Vellum is easier still.

**Value Winner: Atticus**

At $147 versus $500, Atticus provides exceptional value. You get comparable core formatting capabilities plus writing tools for less than a third of Vellum’s price. For budget conscious authors, this difference is often decisive.

**Fiction Formatting Winner: Vellum**

For straightforward novels with standard chapter structures, both work beautifully. Vellum’s templates are slightly more refined and the output is marginally more polished. The difference is subtle, but Vellum maintains an edge for pure fiction formatting excellence.

**Nonfiction Formatting Winner: Atticus**

Atticus handles complex nonfiction requirements better. More robust footnote support, better sidebar handling, and stronger table formatting make it the better choice for nonfiction authors with layout challenges Vellum struggles with.

**Platform Compatibility Winner: Atticus**

Atticus works on Windows and Mac. Vellum only works on Mac. For Windows users, this is not even a competition. Atticus wins by being available.

**Visual Polish Winner: Vellum**

Template quality, interface refinement, and overall aesthetic excellence favor Vellum. The designs are more sophisticated, the customization feels more polished, and the final output has subtle quality touches that make books look just slightly more professional.

**Feature Breadth Winner: Atticus**

Between writing environment, broader nonfiction support, and cloud based access across devices, Atticus offers more features beyond pure formatting. Vellum focuses exclusively on formatting and does it brilliantly, but Atticus provides more tools in one package.

**Reliability Winner: Vellum**

Years of proven performance and refinement give Vellum the edge in reliability. Files consistently work flawlessly across platforms without troubleshooting. Atticus is very reliable, but Vellum’s longer track record earns trust.

## Making Your Decision: Which Tool Fits Your Needs?

Choose Vellum if you use Mac, publish primarily fiction, want the absolute most polished formatting available, value interface elegance and ease of use above all else, or simply want the tool with the longest proven track record. Vellum remains the gold standard for a reason. If you have a Mac and can afford the $500 investment, it delivers exceptional results consistently.

Choose Atticus if you use Windows and want professional formatting previously unavailable to you, publish nonfiction with complex formatting requirements, prefer all in one tools combining writing and formatting, or need to maximize value with a limited budget. Atticus makes professional book production accessible to more authors at a fraction of Vellum’s cost while delivering genuinely good results.

Choose neither if you are publishing one book with simple formatting, have extremely tight budgets, or are comfortable learning free alternatives like Reedsy Book Editor, Google Docs with proper formatting, or platform specific tools from Draft2Digital. These free options work, though they require more manual effort and produce less polished results.

## Beyond the Tools: What Actually Makes Books Successful

Here is the truth formatting software companies will not emphasize: the formatting tool matters far less than your book’s content, cover, and marketing. A brilliant book formatted adequately still finds readers. A poorly written book formatted perfectly still fails. Both Vellum and Atticus produce professional results that readers will not notice unless formatting is done badly.

Invest in professional editing first. No formatting software fixes weak writing, plot holes, or grammatical errors. Invest in a competitive cover design that looks professional alongside traditionally published books in your genre. Invest time in learning effective marketing and building reader relationships.

Then choose whichever formatting tool fits your budget and operating system. Either Vellum or Atticus will serve you well. The difference between them matters less than the difference between professionally formatted books and amateur ones, and both clear that bar easily.

## Your Formatting Decision Starts Here

Vellum and Atticus both represent smart investments for self published authors serious about professional presentation. Neither is wrong. Both will make your books look good and save you significant time compared to manual formatting. The choice comes down to your operating system, budget, specific formatting needs, and personal preferences.

Ready to format your book professionally? Understanding your options is the first step toward publication ready files. Choose the tool that serves your situation best, invest time learning it properly, and create books that look as professional as your writing deserves.

## Why Not Just Use Word or Pages?

**The Short Answer:** Word and Pages can technically format books, but they’re designed for regular documents (letters, reports, essays), not books. It’s like using a butter knife instead of a chef’s knife – you _can_ cut vegetables with it, but it’s frustrating and the results aren’t as good.

**The Real Problems with Word orPages:**

1. **Ebook Conversion is a Nightmare**
   
    + Word docs don’t convert cleanly to proper ebook formats (EPUB, MOBI)
    + You’ll get weird spacing issues, broken formatting, missing chapter breaks
    + Images often don’t display correctly
    + Table of contents won’t be clickable/functional
    + Different devices (Kindle, iPad, Android) will display your book differently and often badly
2. **Print Formatting is Extremely Technical**
   
    + Getting margins, bleeds, and trim sizes exactly right is tedious
    + Page numbers and running headers break constantly
    + Chapter starts need to be on right-hand pages (Word fights you on this)
    + One small change can mess up your entire layout
    + Creating the proper PDF for printing requires specific settings most authors get wrong
3. **Time Investment is Massive**
   
    + What takes 30 minutes in Vellum takes 6-8 hours in Word
    + You need to manually format every single element
    + Troubleshooting errors is frustrating and time-consuming
    + Making changes means reformatting everything again
4. **The Results Look Amateur**
   
    + Readers can tell when a book is “Word formatted”
    + Inconsistent spacing, awkward page breaks, clunky chapter headings
    + Professional books have subtle design elements Word can’t easily replicate

**Real Author Example:** An author spends 8 hours formatting their novel in Word. Uploads to KDP. Gets errors. Fixes them. Uploads again. Some Kindle devices show weird spacing. Readers leave reviews saying “formatting made it hard to read.” Author has to reformat and republish.

Same author using Vellum: 30 minutes. Upload once. Works perfectly on all devices. Readers never mention formatting (which is exactly what you want – invisible formatting means readers focus on your story).

**When Wordor Pages ARE Fine:**

* Very simple books with no special formatting
* You only need ONE format (like just a PDF)
* You have professional design skills and lots of time
* You’re doing a test/proof copy before paying for professional formatting

**The Investment Math:**

* Vellum costs $500
* Professional formatter charges $200-500 PER BOOK
* If you publish 2-3 books, Vellum pays for itself
* If you publish 1 book and your time is worth $50/hour, those 8 saved hours = $400
